🛠 Applied Mechanics

Statics

  • ΣF = 0 (equilibrium: net force is zero) Sum of forces in any direction is zero
  • F = ma (force = mass × acceleration) m = mass (kg), a = acceleration (m/s²)
  • F_action = -F_reaction (Newton’s third law) Equal and opposite forces
  • M = F × d (moment of a force: force × perpendicular distance) F = force (N), d = distance (m)
  • F_friction = μN (friction force: μ = coefficient of friction, N = normal force) μ = friction coefficient, N = normal force (N)
  • μ = tanθ (coefficient of friction: θ = angle of friction or repose) θ = angle of incline
  • x_cg = (Σm_i x_i) / Σm_i (center of gravity for composite body: weighted average of centroids) m_i = mass, x_i = centroid position
  • J = (πr^4)/2 (polar moment of inertia, circular shaft) r = radius (m)
  • τ = (T × r) / J (shear stress in shaft: T = torque, r = radius, J = polar moment) T = torque (Nm), r = radius (m)

Kinematics

  • s = ut + (1/2)at² (distance traversed, uniform acceleration: u = initial velocity, a = acceleration, t = time) u = initial velocity (m/s), a = acceleration (m/s²)
  • v = u + at (velocity) v = final velocity (m/s)
  • s = vt (distance, constant velocity) v = velocity (m/s), t = time (s)
  • R_max = (u²/g) at θ = 45° (projectile motion, max range: u = initial velocity, g = 9.8 m/s²) u = initial velocity (m/s), g = gravity

Dynamics

  • W = Fd cosθ (work: θ = angle between force and displacement) F = force (N), d = displacement (m)
  • KE = (1/2)mv² (kinetic energy) m = mass (kg), v = velocity (m/s)
  • PE = mgh (potential energy: h = height) m = mass (kg), g = 9.8 m/s², h = height (m)
  • P = W/t or P = Fv (power: v = velocity) W = work (J), t = time (s), v = velocity (m/s)
  • T = F × r or T = Iα (torque: I = moment of inertia, α = angular acceleration) F = force (N), r = radius (m), I = moment of inertia (kg.m²)
  • I = (1/4)MR² (moment of inertia, circular disc, about diameter) M = mass (kg), R = radius (m)
  • P = Tω (power from torque: ω = angular velocity, rad/s) T = torque (Nm), ω = angular velocity (rad/s)
  • m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ (conservation of momentum, isolated system) m = mass (kg), u/v = velocities (m/s)
  • Total energy (KE + PE) = constant (conservation of energy) KE = kinetic, PE = potential energy
  • δ_max = (WL^3)/(48EI) (maximum deflection, simply supported beam, central load: W = load, L = length, E = Young’s modulus, I = moment of inertia) W = load (N), L = length (m), E = modulus (Pa)

🔥 Thermodynamics

Gas Laws

  • PV = nRT (ideal gas law: P = pressure, V = volume, n = moles, R = 8.314 J/mol.K, T = Kelvin) P = pressure (Pa), V = volume (m³), T = temperature (K)
  • P₁V₁ = P₂V₂ (Boyle’s law, constant T) Constant temperature process
  • V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂ (Charles’ law, constant P) Constant pressure process
  • (P₁V₁)/T₁ = (P₂V₂)/T₂ (combined gas law) Relates P, V, T changes

First Law

  • ΔU = Q - W (first law: ΔU = change in internal energy, Q = heat added, W = work done by system) Q = heat (J), W = work (J)
  • ΔU = nC_vΔT (internal energy, ideal gas: C_v = specific heat at constant volume) n = moles, C_v = specific heat (J/mol.K)
  • W = PΔV (work done, constant pressure) P = pressure (Pa), ΔV = volume change (m³)

Second Law

  • η = 1 - (T_cold/T_hot) (Carnot efficiency, T in Kelvin) T_cold, T_hot = temperatures (K)
  • ΔS ≥ 0 (entropy change, isolated system: S = entropy) S = entropy (J/K)
  • η = W/Q_in (thermal efficiency: W = work output, Q_in = heat input) W = work (J), Q_in = heat input (J)

Processes

  • Isothermal: W = nRT ln(V₂/V₁), ΔU = 0 (constant T) Constant temperature process
  • Adiabatic: PV^γ = constant, W = C_v (T₂ - T₁), T₁V₁^(γ-1) = T₂V₂^(γ-1) (no heat transfer, γ = C_p/C_v, e.g., 1.4 for diatomic gas) γ = adiabatic index
  • Isochoric: W = 0, ΔU = Q = nC_vΔT (constant V) Constant volume process
  • Isobaric: W = P (V₂ - V₁), Q = nC_pΔT (constant P: C_p = specific heat at constant pressure) Constant pressure process
  • Polytropic: PV^n = constant, W = (P₂V₂ - P₁V₁)/(1 - n) n = polytropic index

Cycles

  • Otto: η = 1 - (1/r^(γ-1)) (r = compression ratio) r = compression ratio
  • Diesel: η = 1 - (1/r^(γ-1)) × [(r_c^γ - 1)/(γ(r_c - 1))] (r_c = cut-off ratio) r_c = cut-off ratio

Additional

  • C_p - C_v = R (relationship between specific heats) C_p, C_v = specific heats (J/mol.K), R = 8.314 J/mol.K

🚗 Heat Engines

Cycle Efficiencies

  • Otto: η = 1 - (1/r^(γ-1)) (r = compression ratio, γ = adiabatic index, e.g., 1.4 for air) r = compression ratio, γ = 1.4
  • Diesel: η = 1 - (1/r^(γ-1)) × [(r_c^γ - 1)/(γ(r_c - 1))] (r_c = cut-off ratio) r_c = cut-off ratio
  • Brayton: η = 1 - (1/r_p^((γ-1)/γ)) (r_p = pressure ratio) r_p = pressure ratio
  • r = V_total / V_clearance (compression ratio) V_total, V_clearance = volumes (m³)
  • r_c = V_cut-off / V_clearance (cut-off ratio, Diesel) V_cut-off, V_clearance = volumes (m³)

Work and Power

  • W = Q_in - Q_out (work output: Q_in = heat added, Q_out = heat rejected) Q_in, Q_out = heat (J)
  • P = W/t or P = Tω (power output: T = torque, ω = angular velocity, rad/s) W = work (J), T = torque (Nm)
  • IP = (P_m × L × A × N × n)/(60) (indicated power: P_m = mean effective pressure, L = stroke length, A = piston area, N = RPM, n = number of cylinders; for 4-stroke, divide by 2) P_m = pressure (Pa), L = length (m)
  • BP = (2πNT)/60 (brake power: N = RPM, T = brake torque) N = RPM, T = torque (Nm)
  • MEP = W_net / V_d (mean effective pressure: V_d = displacement volume) W_net = net work (J), V_d = volume (m³)

Efficiency Metrics

  • η_mech = BP/IP (mechanical efficiency) BP = brake power (W), IP = indicated power (W)
  • η_th = W/Q_in (thermal efficiency) W = work (J), Q_in = heat input (J)
  • SFC = Fuel consumption (kg/h) / BP (kW) (specific fuel consumption) Fuel consumption (kg/h), BP (kW)

⚡️ Electro-Mechanical

  • P = VI cosφ (electrical power: V = voltage, I = current, cosφ = power factor) V = voltage (V), I = current (A)
  • P_mech = Tω (mechanical power, motor: T = torque, ω = angular velocity) T = torque (Nm), ω = rad/s
  • η = (P_out / P_in) × 100 (motor efficiency: P_out = P_mech, P_in = P_elec) P_out, P_in = power (W)
  • η = (P_elec / P_mech) × 100 (generator efficiency) P_elec, P_mech = power (W)
  • T = (P × 60) / (2πN) (torque: P = power in watts, N = RPM) P = power (W), N = RPM
  • E_b = V - I_a R_a (back EMF, DC motor: V = applied voltage, I_a = armature current, R_a = armature resistance) V = voltage (V), I_a = current (A)
  • N_s = (120f) / p (synchronous speed, AC motor: f = frequency in Hz, p = number of poles) f = frequency (Hz), p = poles
  • P_cu = I²R (copper loss: I = current, R = resistance) I = current (A), R = resistance (Ω)
  • s = (N_s - N_r) / N_s (slip, induction motor: N_s = synchronous speed, N_r = rotor speed) N_s, N_r = speeds (RPM)
  • C = (P (tanφ₁ - tanφ₂)) / (2πf V²) (power factor correction: C = capacitance, P = power, φ₁/φ₂ = initial/final phase angles) C = capacitance (F), P = power (W)

🏭 Industrial Boiler

  • η_b = (m_s (h_s - h_f)) / (m_f × CV) (thermal efficiency: m_s = steam mass, h_s = steam enthalpy, h_f = feedwater enthalpy, m_f = fuel mass, CV = calorific value) m_s, m_f = mass (kg), h_s, h_f = enthalpy (kJ/kg)
  • η_overall = (Steam energy output / Fuel energy input) × 100 (overall efficiency) Energy in kJ
  • Q = m_f × CV (heat supplied) m_f = fuel mass (kg), CV = calorific value (kJ/kg)
  • Q_loss = m_g × C_p × (T_g - T_a) (heat loss, flue gas: m_g = flue gas mass, C_p = specific heat, T_g = flue gas temperature, T_a = ambient temperature) m_g = mass (kg), C_p = specific heat (kJ/kg.K)
  • E = (m_s (h_s - h_f)) / 2257 (equivalent evaporation: 2257 kJ/kg = latent heat of steam at 100°C, 1 atm) m_s = steam mass (kg), h_s, h_f = enthalpy (kJ/kg)
  • Capacity = m_s / t (boiler capacity: kg/s or kg/h) m_s = steam mass (kg), t = time (s or h)
  • ΔP = h × ρ × g (draft pressure: h = chimney height, ρ = air density, g = 9.8 m/s²) h = height (m), ρ = density (kg/m³)
  • m_f = (m_s (h_s - h_f)) / (η_b × CV) (fuel consumption rate) m_s, m_f = mass (kg), η_b = efficiency

💰 Estimating and Costing

  • Q_be = FC / (P - VC) (break-even point, units: FC = fixed cost, P = selling price per unit, VC = variable cost per unit) FC, P, VC = costs ($)
  • S_be = FC / (1 - (VC/P)) (break-even sales, value) FC, VC, P = costs ($)
  • ROI = (Net Profit / Investment) × 100 (return on investment) Net Profit, Investment = $
  • NP = TR - TC (net profit: TR = total revenue, TC = total cost) TR, TC = $
  • D = (C - S) / n (straight-line depreciation: C = initial cost, S = salvage value, n = useful life in years) C, S = $, n = years
  • R = D / C × 100 (annual depreciation rate) D = depreciation ($), C = cost ($)
  • TC = FC + VC × Q (total cost: Q = quantity produced) FC, VC = costs ($), Q = units
  • WC = Current Assets - Current Liabilities (working capital) Assets, Liabilities = $
  • A = P / [(1 - (1 + r)^(-n)) / r] (amortization: P = principal, r = interest rate, n = periods) P = principal ($), r = rate
  • CM = P - VC (contribution margin) P, VC = costs ($ per unit)
  • PM = (NP / TR) × 100 (profit margin) NP = net profit ($), TR = revenue ($)

📏 Machine Drawing

  • T = Upper Limit - Lower Limit (tolerance range) Limits in mm
  • C = Hole Dimension - Shaft Dimension (fit clearance, for clearance fit) Dimensions in mm
  • A = πr² (area of circle, for isometric views) r = radius (m)
